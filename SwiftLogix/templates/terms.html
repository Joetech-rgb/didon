Legal Terms Based on Ghanaian Logistics Law

1. Regulatory Registration

SwiftLogix and all associated shipping service providers comply with the Ghana Shippers Authority Act, 2024, including mandatory registration with the Authority and publication in the Shippersâ€™ Bulletin. Unregistered entities are not permitted to operate or provide logistics services in Ghana.

2. Ship Registration & Liability

In accordance with the Ghana Shipping Act, 2003 (Act 645), any vessel operating in Ghanaian waters must hold a valid registration certificate. Vessels not properly registered may be detained, and owners may face fines.

3. Negotiated Charges & Standards

Charges for shipping, port handling, customs, and related services shall be negotiated between service providers and the Ghana Shippers Authority, following regulations under LI 2190 (2012). Overcharges may result in administrative penalties and mandatory refunds. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

4. Local Vessel Operation (Cabotage)

Under LI 2438 (2021), vessels engaging in local trade must hold a cabotage certificate. Dispute resolution is provided via a designated Dispute Committee with appeal rights up to the High Court.

5. Dispute & Complaint Resolution

SwiftLogix abides by statutory dispute resolution frameworks, providing clients with mechanisms aligned with Ghanaian law for handling complaints, claims, and arbitration in case of service disputes.
